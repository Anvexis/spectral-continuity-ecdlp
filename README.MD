# Spectral Continuity in Elliptic Curve Scalar Multiplication

> Experimental investigation of local spectral structure in secp256k1 scalar multiplication.

## Overview

This repository explores a novel hypothesis regarding the behavior of elliptic curve scalar multiplication on the Bitcoin curve **secp256k1**.

The central observation is that the sequence of points generated by:

[
P_k = kG
]

exhibits measurable **local continuity in the spectral domain** when analyzing the x-coordinate sequence using Fourier-based techniques.

While elliptic curve discrete logarithms are generally modeled as pseudorandom from a practical cryptographic perspective, the experiments in this repository suggest that neighboring scalar values may produce spectral signatures that evolve smoothly rather than randomly.

---

## Motivation

The Elliptic Curve Discrete Logarithm Problem (ECDLP) is the foundation of Bitcoin and many modern cryptographic systems.

Traditional understanding assumes that scalar multiplication behaves sufficiently randomly to prevent exploitation of local structure.

This project investigates whether:

* Nearby scalars produce related spectral signatures
* Fourier amplitudes evolve continuously
* Spectral phases exhibit predictable motion
* Spectral distances can serve as a heuristic similarity metric

The goal is **not** to break secp256k1, but to study previously unexplored mathematical properties of scalar multiplication.

# Experimental Results

## 1. Spectrum Randomness Analysis

Example output:

```text
Mean amplitude (first 50 freqs): 0.0353
Std deviation: 0.0173
Std/Mean ratio: 0.4905

Expected for white noise: ~1.0

RESULT:
Spectrum is NOT white noise
```

Observation:

* The spectrum is significantly more structured than ideal white noise.
* Amplitudes are unevenly distributed.
* Dominant modes appear consistently.

```text
Offset     Spectral Distance
```

Observation:

* Spectral distance increases gradually.

## 3. Amplitude Correlation

Example result:

```text

```

Interpretation:

* Consecutive scalar values produce highly correlated amplitude spectra.
* The observed correlation is far above what would be expected for independent random sequences.

## 4. Phase Evolution

Example result:

```text
Mean phase change: 0.3195 rad
Std: 0.1339 rad

Expected random walk:
~1.8138 rad
```

Observation:

* Spectral phases evolve smoothly.
* Neighboring scalars produce predictable phase shifts.
* The behavior resembles a continuous dynamical system rather than a random process.

---

## 5. Spectral Gradient Descent

A simple gradient-descent experiment was performed on a reduced keyspace.

Result:

```text
FAILED to recover k
```

This is expected.

The experiment demonstrates:

* The spectral landscape contains gradients.
* The landscape is non-convex.
* Local minima prevent naive optimization.

Therefore:

* Structure exists.
* Simple gradient descent is insufficient.

# Hypothesis

The experiments support the following hypothesis:

> The spectral signature associated with scalar multiplication on secp256k1 behaves as a locally continuous function of the scalar value.

Formally:

[
|S(kG)-S((k+\Delta)G)|
]

tends to grow smoothly for small values of Δ.

This suggests the existence of local geometric structure in the spectral representation of elliptic curve points.

---

# Mathematical Perspective

Scalar multiplication can be viewed as a discrete dynamical system:

[
T_G(P)=P+G
]

acting on the cyclic subgroup generated by G.

The corresponding coordinate sequence:

[
x(kG)
]

may be analyzed using Fourier methods.

This repository investigates whether low-frequency spectral modes encode information about local movement along the orbit generated by repeated additions of G.

# Limitations

Important limitations:

* No polynomial-time ECDLP algorithm is presented.
* No practical attack on Bitcoin is demonstrated.
* Local continuity does not imply global invertibility.
* Gradient descent alone fails due to local minima.
* Observed structure may not translate into asymptotic complexity improvements.

These experiments should be viewed as exploratory mathematical research.
